## The Grandfather's Rule

**1989, age 10, fixing a VCR:**

> "You must only fix with bounded moves."

Why was he so strict? Why couldn't I just move the head wherever I wanted?

**Because reality is local.**

A VCR is a physical system. You can't teleport the head across the tape. You can't make unbounded moves. The machine doesn't allow it. Physics doesn't allow it.

He wasn't teaching me repair. He was teaching me the fundamental constraint of all physical computation:

**Bounded moves are the only real moves.**

## The Implementations (2004-2020)

Each apparent breakthrough was the same insight in different clothing:

**2004 - Polish Notation**: Structure in representation reveals structure in computation.

**2014 - Dual Tree**: Agents finding optimal paths through apparently exponential spaces. How? Bounded local moves.

**2020 - Sample Space**: The distinction that unlocked everything:
- S_complete = all syntactically valid states (exponential)
- S_observable = states reachable by bounded moves (polynomial)

The "hard" problems were searching S_complete. The "tractable" problems were searching S_observable.

**They were always the same problems with different search spaces.**
