| 2nd | μ⁻, νμ | c, s | Unstable |
| 3rd | τ⁻, ντ | t, b | Very unstable |

**Why three?** No theoretical explanation. The number 3 is arbitrary.


**Evidence:**

   - e → μ: 207× (1st resonance)
   - e → τ: 3477× (2nd resonance)
   - u → c: ~500× (1st resonance)
   - u → t: ~75000× (2nd resonance)

### Prediction

**ARC Prediction:** The "three generations" are S_complete artifacts. In bounded theory:
- Only 1st generation particles are fundamental (REAL)
- 2nd and 3rd generations are excited states (resonances)
- This reduces 18 particles to 6 + energy levels
