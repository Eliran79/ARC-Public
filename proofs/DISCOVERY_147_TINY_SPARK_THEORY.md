## 2. Rashi's Proof: The Water Was Already There

Rashi (Rabbi Shlomo Yitzhaki, 1040-1105) on בְּרֵאשִׁית:

> **אֵין הַמִּקְרָא הַזֶּה אוֹמֵר אֶלָּא דָּרְשֵׁנִי** — "This verse says nothing but 'Interpret me!'"

Rashi proves that בְּרֵאשִׁית is a construct state — "In the beginning **of** creating" — not "In the beginning, [He] created." The text describes a **process**, not an instant.

Critical observation from Rashi:

> **וְעַדַיִין לֹא גִלָּה הַמִּקְרָא, בְּרִיאַת הַמַּיִם מָתַי הָיְתָה** — "And yet the text has not revealed WHEN the water was created!"

**The water was already there before creation.** The Torah explicitly states that the creation narrative does NOT begin from nothing. There was a pre-existing fluid (מַיִם — water).

> "Why does it say 'One'? Because the Holy One was alone in His world."

"Day One" (יוֹם אֶחָד) — not "First Day" (יוֹם רִאשׁוֹן). The text emphasizes **oneness** — a single, unified initial state. One trigger. One spark. The Inverse Landauer Principle: one bit cascades to polynomial structure.
